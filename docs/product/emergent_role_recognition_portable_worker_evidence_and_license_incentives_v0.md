# Emergent Role Recognition, Portable Worker Evidence, And License Incentives v0

Status: current professional-product pressure  
Date: 2026-07-15  
Authority posture: professional product interpretation only; not canonical Cadence doctrine, implementation truth, employment policy, compensation policy, legal advice, final pricing, packaging commitment, schema authority, or Dev tasking

## 1. Purpose

This document preserves three connected professional-product pressures:

1. **A person's actual work may reveal a role shape that differs from the title assigned before the work began.**
2. **Professional work may create legitimate capability evidence that should continue to benefit the worker after the project or employment relationship ends, without exporting protected organizational data.**
3. **Cadence should prefer positive product-family incentives over punitive feature gatekeeping when encouraging organizations to use professional rather than personal products for company work.**

## 2. Assigned role versus emergent role

Traditional role design often starts with a box:

```text
ROLE_TITLE
  -> EXPECTED_RESPONSIBILITIES
  -> PEOPLE_EXPECTED_TO_FIT_THE_BOX
```

Real work often runs in reverse:

```text
ACTUAL_TASKS
  -> REPEATED_CONTRIBUTIONS
  -> RESPONSIBILITY_PATTERN
  -> CAPABILITY_EVIDENCE
  -> EMERGENT_ROLE_SHAPE
```

A worker may repeatedly:

- solve problems outside the formal job description;
- train peers;
- bridge teams;
- coordinate vendors;
- preserve institutional memory;
- create documentation;
- lead without title authority;
- perform analysis;
- cover gaps across several functions;
- become the trusted troubleshooter;
- take on invisible coordination or translation work;
- demonstrate adjacent capability that makes a future task easier to learn.

The professional product should be able to recognize this pattern without pretending that a machine can automatically change a title, compensation, employment status, or organizational authority.

## 3. Emergent role recognition

A future professional capability may compare:

- assigned role;
- official responsibilities;
- actual task history;
- recurring contribution classes;
- stretch work;
- temporary emergency coverage;
- cross-functional work;
- capability evidence;
- peer or manager review;
- workload concentration;
- duration and recency.

Possible outputs include:

- `wearing many hats` recognition;
- practical role summaries;
- role-shape drift;
- under-recognized responsibility clusters;
- hidden bridge functions;
- repeated work outside formal scope;
- responsibilities that may need staffing, recognition, authority, compensation review, or workload reduction;
- candidate dynamic job descriptions.

The preferred model is:

```text
ASSIGNED_ROLE
  + EXPLAINABLE_WORK_EVIDENCE
  + CONTRIBUTION_PATTERN
  + HUMAN_REVIEW
  -> EMERGENT_ROLE_DESCRIPTION
```

Not:

```text
BLACK_BOX_ACTIVITY_MONITORING
  -> AUTOMATIC_ROLE_LABEL
  -> AUTOMATIC_PERFORMANCE_SCORE
```

## 4. Worker recognition is a product goal

The system should not exist only to help employers extract more work.

A grounded record of out-of-role contribution may support conversations about:

- recognition;
- promotions;
- raises;
- workload reduction;
- staffing needs;
- corrected titles;
- professional development;
- internal mobility;
- succession planning;
- mentorship;
- job redesign.

The system must not claim that evidence guarantees any employment outcome.

It should also guard against the exploitative pattern:

```text
YOU_PROVED_YOU_CAN_DO_MORE
therefore
WE_WILL_GIVE_YOU_MORE_WITHOUT_SUPPORT
```

Role recognition should be capable of surfacing overload and hidden labor, not merely increasing utilization.

## 5. Dynamic job descriptions

A dynamic job description may be a reviewed, evidence-linked representation of what a role became in practice.

It should distinguish:

- official expectations;
- actual recurring responsibilities;
- temporary coverage;
- occasional stretch work;
- cross-functional contribution;
- newly demonstrated capability;
- hidden labor;
- responsibilities that should be reassigned or staffed separately.

Possible employer value:

- more honest recruiting;
- better onboarding;
- clearer internal mobility;
- recognition of unrealistic job bundles;
- better succession planning;
- clearer evidence for new headcount;
- more accurate descriptions of roles that evolved over time.

A generated description must remain reviewable, attributable, and contestable.

## 6. Capability matching from real work

When the system asks `Who can do this job?`, titles and manually declared skills should not be the only evidence.

Future matching may consider:

- formal qualification;
- verified task classes;
- repeated adjacent experience;
- project outcomes;
- review or feedback;
- recency;
- context similarity;
- confidence and uncertainty;
- demonstrated near-ability.

A worker might surface because:

```text
FORMALLY_QUALIFIED
or
DEMONSTRATED_CAPABILITY
or
ADJACENT_EVIDENCE_WITH_SHALLOW_LEARNING_CURVE
```

The system should explain:

- why the person surfaced;
- what evidence supports the match;
- what evidence is missing;
- what learning gap remains;
- whether the recommendation is confident or provisional.

## 7. Worker-owned experience continuity

The person continues after a job, project, or workspace ends.

Professional Cadence should distinguish:

- organization-owned operational records;
- confidential or proprietary artifacts;
- employer-controlled task systems;
- public work products;
- verified accomplishments;
- attestations;
- the worker's lived experience and capability history;
- the worker's personal identity interpretation.

The product pressure is:

```text
ORGANIZATION_MAY_CONTROL_THE_WORKSPACE_AND_RECORDS
PERSON_STILL_LIVED_THE_EXPERIENCE
PERSON_SHOULD_NOT_BECOME_HISTORYLESS_WHEN_ACCESS_ENDS
```

This is not a legal ownership conclusion.

The product should support a privacy-aware, provenance-aware path for legitimate portable evidence without copying employer secrets into a personal account.

## 8. Possible portable evidence forms

Future portable forms may include:

- manager or peer attestations;
- verified task categories;
- verified project participation;
- duration summaries;
- accomplishment statements;
- role-shape summaries;
- capability claims with confidence and provenance;
- evidence of adjacent or near-ability;
- worker-written reflections that contain no protected source;
- future cryptographic verification if justified.

A person should be able to preserve `I did this work` without automatically receiving the underlying confidential documents that prove every detail.

## 9. Professional-to-personal continuity

A future cross-product path may look like:

```text
PROFESSIONAL_WORKSPACE
  -> real tasks and contributions occur
  -> bounded evidence is created
  -> worker can review or contest derived capability claims
  -> appropriate portable evidence crosses to personal Cadence
  -> protected organizational source does not cross by default
  -> personal identity remains person-controlled
```

The employer must not gain access to the worker's personal account, private reflections, family context, health context, personal AI memory, or unrelated life material merely because a professional license exists.

The worker must not silently export organization-owned source or confidential material merely because they contributed to it.

## 10. Low-ceremony projects and malleable roles

The lightweight ad-hoc project mode should not require a formal role title before participation begins.

A temporary group may start with:

- purpose;
- participants;
- visibility;
- tasks;
- artifacts;
- decisions;
- lightweight responsibilities;
- closeout condition.

Role recognition may remain descriptive rather than authoritative:

```text
Sam keeps coordinating vendors and documenting decisions.
Jordan is becoming the bridge between engineering and operations.
Priya has repeatedly handled accessibility review with assistance.
```

These observations may be useful even when nobody wants formal titles.

## 11. Carrot-based product guardrails

Cadence should discourage long-term organizational use of personal products for company work.

Pure feature gating may feel punitive without solving the incentive problem.

A better product-family pressure is to make the correct professional environment economically and personally attractive.

Candidate concept:

```text
PERSONAL_PLUS = x per person
PROFESSIONAL = y per person

ACTIVE_PROFESSIONAL_LICENSE
  -> eligible worker receives a discount or included benefit toward PERSONAL_PLUS
  -> benefit persists while professional eligibility is active
  -> personal account remains private and person-owned
```

The exact prices, percentages, eligibility rules, and package names are unresolved.

## 12. Why the carrot matters

Potential benefits:

- workers prefer the professional environment because it also benefits their personal product;
- organizations face less pressure to misuse personal accounts for company coordination;
- personal and professional continuity becomes an advantage rather than a forced merge;
- workers can retain person-owned identity and experience evidence;
- the company gets the governance and trust-domain controls it actually needs;
- the product avoids relying only on artificial restrictions.

## 13. Guardrails for the incentive model

A professional-linked personal benefit must not mean:

- the employer owns the personal account;
- the employer can inspect personal data;
- the worker loses personal history when employment ends;
- the worker loses legitimate portable experience evidence when the discount ends;
- professional and personal AI memory merge automatically;
- the company can require private personal context as a condition of employment;
- the company can silently disable the worker's independent personal account.

Preferred principle:

```text
BENEFIT_CAN_END
PERSONAL_ACCOUNT_CONTINUES
PERSONAL_DATA_REMAINS_PERSON_CONTROLLED
PORTABLE_EXPERIENCE_DOES_NOT_DISAPPEAR
PROFESSIONAL_SECRETS_DO_NOT_CROSS_BY_DEFAULT
```

## 14. Risks and anti-patterns

Guard against:

- surveillance disguised as role recognition;
- measuring only digitally visible work;
- penalizing workers whose important work is less instrumented;
- treating emergency coverage as permanent willingness;
- using inferred capability to assign more work without consent;
- using inferred roles as automatic performance judgments;
- exposing worker evidence to people without legitimate access;
- extracting private personal identity into employer systems;
- exporting confidential employer material into personal systems;
- locking workers into Cadence to preserve their history;
- making benefit eligibility dependent on surrendering personal privacy.
