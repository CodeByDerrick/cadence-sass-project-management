# Cadence SaaS Project Management

Cadence SaaS Project Management is the organizational, multi-user professional extension of the Cadence product family for coordinated project execution.

It is not a generic task tracker with an AI chat box added on top, and it is not the sole owner of Cadence project-management theory. The professional product extends a shared Cadence foundation for projects, tasks, dependencies, scheduling, scenarios, constraints, projections, conversational manipulation, and lenses with multi-user staffing, organizational authority, permissions, approvals, cost, overtime, policy, and governance.

## Lightweight ad-hoc professional mode

Not every professional project needs to feel like a formal department or heavily governed program.

The current professional product pressure explicitly includes:

```text
TEMPORARY_GROUP != PERMANENT_TEAM
PROJECT_PARTICIPANT != STAFFING_OBJECT
PROFESSIONAL_MULTI_USER != MAXIMUM_ENTERPRISE_CEREMONY
```

A large company may assemble a temporary cross-functional `ragtag group of super buddies` to solve one unusual problem. A nonprofit may form a special committee. A small organization may need shared project truth without resource optimization, cost models, or formal approval chains.

The professional product should be able to start with a lightweight surface—purpose, participants, selected visibility, tasks, dependencies, artifacts, decisions, and closeout—and add stronger governance only when justified.

See:

- `docs/product/lightweight_ad_hoc_project_mode_v0.md`

## Emergent role recognition and worker evidence

People frequently work outside the boxes defined before the work begins.

The professional product should be able to distinguish:

```text
ASSIGNED_ROLE
from
ACTUAL_TASK_AND_CONTRIBUTION_PATTERN
from
EMERGENT_ROLE_DESCRIPTION
```

A person may be wearing many hats, repeatedly performing hidden bridge work, training peers, covering functions beyond title scope, or demonstrating near-ability for a future task. These patterns can support recognition, staffing, workload, promotion, compensation, job-design, recruiting, and career-development conversations without becoming automatic employment judgments.

The continuing person should also be able to retain legitimate experience evidence when a project or employment relationship ends, while confidential organization-owned records remain protected.

See:

- `docs/product/emergent_role_recognition_portable_worker_evidence_and_license_incentives_v0.md`

## Positive-incentive product guardrails

Cadence should discourage organizations from using personal products as long-term professional systems.

One candidate product-family pressure is:

```text
ACTIVE_PROFESSIONAL_LICENSE
  -> worker receives discount or benefit toward PERSONAL_PLUS
  -> personal account remains person-owned and private
  -> benefit may end when eligibility ends
  -> personal history and legitimate portable experience remain
```

This is not final pricing or packaging. The goal is to make the correct professional environment more attractive through carrots rather than relying only on irritating restrictions.

## Personal / professional trust-domain boundary

This repo also inherits:

- `CodeByDerrick/cadence-strategy:docs/doctrine/personal_professional_continuity_and_trust_domains_v0.md`

Current implications include:

- personal and professional Cadence may coexist on the same mobile device;
- data, artifacts, memory, permissions, ownership, and AI context must not bleed across trust domains by default;
- same-device availability does not imply cross-domain permission;
- Personal -> Professional Light -> multi-user Professional is a supported conceptual lifecycle;
- reverse downgrade from multi-user Professional is a governed administrative event;
- mixed-context brain dumps may contain both personal and professional meaning and should not be forced into false binary classification;
- offboarding should distinguish the continuing person from organizational access and organization-owned records;
- appropriate portable experience may continue with the person without exporting protected source;
- work-managed devices may provide strong policy boundaries but do not replace domain-aware identity and access control.

No claim that these behaviors are implemented is made here.

## Foundational product posture

The current direction assumes:

- **Mobile first and voice first, not mobile only or voice only.** Professional use may be more desktop-heavy because portfolio planning benefits from screen space, but capture, questions, approvals, and urgent reprioritization should remain continuously accessible.
- **Simulation before mutation.** Proposed changes to schedules and assignments should be modeled as scenarios before any live plan is altered.
- **Human authorization before consequential execution.** The AI may propose, explain, and hold a strategy, but organizational authority remains explicit.
- **Constraint-aware planning.** Skills, task eligibility, dependencies, PTO, deadlines, overtime limits, costs, policy, and competing project priorities are first-class professional inputs.
- **Progressive governance.** Use the smallest sufficient control surface and add stronger approval, policy, audit, staffing, cost, or risk machinery only when real consequence requires it.
- **Recognition without exploitation.** Emergent role evidence should surface hidden labor and role drift, not become an excuse to assign more work without support.
- **Worker continuity.** A person should not become historyless when organizational access ends.
- **Organizational throughput without hidden harm.** Portfolio optimization should not hide displaced work, violated constraints, burnout pressure, or fairness risks.
- **Explainable gaps and tradeoffs.** Idle periods, delays, blocked work, and downstream effects should be inspectable and explainable in plain language.
- **Artifact-centered operational truth.** Projects, tasks, decisions, approvals, scenarios, staffing constraints, and execution changes should remain durable and attributable rather than ephemeral chat state.
- **Privacy-aware AI routing.** The broader Cadence direction favors local-first reasoning where practical, privacy-preserving context preparation, domain-scoped AI context, and selective escalation to cloud models when stronger capability is justified.

## Current foundation

The first scenario captured here describes a project manager who is asked to expedite an urgent project. Through a voice-first interface, the manager asks Cadence to isolate the project, simulate increasingly aggressive acceleration plans, account for staff capability, PTO, overtime, cost, deadlines, and effects on other projects, hold a selected strategy pending approval, and execute reassignment only after authorization.

The scenario remains valid as the first strong professional orchestration scenario. Its shared project/task/dependency/scheduling/scenario primitives are now explicitly treated as inherited Cadence core; staffing, organizational permissions, approvals, cost, overtime, and governance are the professional extension.

A second professional pressure is explicit: temporary committees, tiger teams, task forces, and one-off cross-functional groups should collaborate with low ceremony and malleable project-scoped roles, adding formal governance only when justified.

A third professional pressure is now explicit: repeated real work may reveal an emergent role, capability, or hidden labor pattern that should be explainable to both worker and organization, capable of improving matching and job design, and potentially portable as bounded person-owned experience evidence without exposing protected organization-owned source.
